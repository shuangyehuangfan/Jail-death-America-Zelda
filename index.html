Introduction

This website is dedicated to the analysis and study of Jail Deaths in America, based on the Reuters report "Inside America's Jails".

The dataset used for this page provides an in-depth analysis and graphical representation of various aspects of the US prison system, using the theme of US prison deaths from 2008 to 2019. It explores factors such as inmate numbers, deaths, staffing and other relevant information. The report uses data from multiple sources, including government reports, official statistics and research conducted by Reuters.

The aim of this study is to focus on and analyse the data focused on deaths in prison due to suicide and deaths due to drugs or alcohol. Our goal is to shed light on this important issue and contribute to efforts to avoid preventable deaths in prisons.

Key Statistics

A comprehensive analysis of data on prisoner deaths due to suicides in US jails from 2008 to 2019 was conducted, encompassing a total of 523 jails surveyed. The study revealed that during this period, a total of 7,571 deaths occurred across these jails, with an average inmate population of 445,106.

Of these deaths, suicides accounted for a significant portion, totaling 2,070 cases.

In the selected data set, 618 or 8.35% were drug and alcohol overdose-related deaths.
